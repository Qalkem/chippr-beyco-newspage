The different forms of blockchain

And what kind of blockchain we use on our Beyco platform

In our blockchain blog series, we take you along with us into the different aspects of blockchain technology. The series explains blockchain in simple steps, looking at the technology from different perspectives. The first blog introduces blockchain, simply describing what it is from a broader perspective. The second blog dives deeper into the primary mechanism of blockchain, explaining blocks, hashes and ultimately why it is considered such a safe technology. As we now know a little bit more about the basics of blockchain, for this third blog we dive deeper into the different forms of blockchain. This will help you understand what type of blockchain we use for our trading platform Beyco, and of course, why. Different forms? Are there multiple forms of blockchain? Yes! There is no such thing as one type of blockchain. Let us elaborate… Broadly speaking, there are three common varieties of blockchain: public, private and permissioned blockchains. Let’s start with the public blockchain. A public blockchain enables anyone and everyone to join and contribute to the network. Bitcoin is the most well-known cryptocurrency that runs on a public blockchain. Basically, Bitcoin is a digital currency that operates without a central bank. Normally, we have federal banks that are responsible for our monetary systems. Now Bitcoin does not have a single person or organization in charge! Bitcoin was created with the idea that everyone joining Bitcoin could “be their own bank” and no middlemen would be needed. This is the idea of a public blockchain: a public network where anyone can join whenever they want. There are no restrictions when it comes to participation. Next to Bitcoin, Ethereum is another example of a popular cryptocurrency that runs on a public blockchain. People appreciate a public blockchain for its true decentralization and anonymity as a user. Also, as everyone has a copy of the ledger on their computer, it creates a distributed nature as well. So, what is the difference with a private blockchain? With a private blockchain, only a single organization has authority over the network. So, it is not open for the public to join in. Private blockchains are mostly used for internal networking of a company and are considered not much different from a regular database.The third category of blockchains is permissioned blockchains. This type of blockchain can be seen as a mix between a public and private blockchain, with many customization options. For example, either anyone is allowed to join after verification of identity, or special permissions are given exclusively to parties performing certain activities. Permissioned blockchains have increased in popularity for supply chains, because of the ability to give specific permissions to each user on the network. So, for instance, certain participants may be given the authorization to see particular information, while others are allowed to view a broader range of information. This gives businesses participating in the blockchain the security to share certain information with others that they only want that party to see. Moreover, this blockchain type enables businesses to comply with international data protection or privacy rules! Now that we know more about these three types, what does this have to do with Beyco? If a blockchain network is used for coffee trading from origin to cup, the process involves multiple parties. Producers, exporters, importers, traders, roasters and service providers all serve a specific role to get the coffee from country of origin to the end consumer. Also, when trade finance is connected, financiers are also involved in the chain. In this case, a permissioned network is the best fit for a platform like Beyco. Why? First, the permissioned blockchain allows for verification of registrations. Beyco looks at every new registration and performs a Customer Due Diligence to make sure we facilitate a platform of trusted parties. This explains the name permissioned blockchain! It allows the facilitator to give permission for users to participate in the platform. Second, it makes sure every user is the owner of its own data. The permissioned blockchain enables users to record, own and manage their own data. Beyco is solely the independent non-profit facilitator of the platform. So, instead of a private blockchain in which one party records all the information, the permissioned blockchain network enables every user, whether this is a producer organization, roaster or trader, to themselves be the manager of their data. Third, it enables users to choose what information they would like to share and whatnot. So, users can choose to publicly share a contract or to have the contract only visible between the two parties who sign the contract. In this way, Beyco enables the recording of personal data and individual contracts between two parties. Details of such a contract are defined between both the buyer and seller.
